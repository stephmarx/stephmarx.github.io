Conflict Analysis

Sunday, January 25, 2015
Describe a time I had a conflict. What was it about? What happened?

Without giving too much detail, I had a friend who made a racist remark. This friend sometimes said things that were inappropriate, while thinking that they weren't "really" inappropriate. In this case, I called the friend out, saying "not cool." They objected, saying that they didn't understand what was offensive about it, that "It's true, though! It's not racist because it's a fact!" Another friend and I explained that, no, it wasn't a fact and was really not okay. We politely explained as best we could, while trying to be still empathetic with the other person.

What was the source of the conflict?

The source was myself and a friend disagreeing about what is offensive and inappropriate.

What basic emotions did you feel upon analyzing the conflict?

At the time, I felt frustrated and embarassed. I wanted to educate my friend without hurting their feelings too badly. I wanted to change their mind and help them understand.

What actions did you take to resolve your conflict?

I involved an ally and tried to communicate rationally and calmly.

If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything?

Perhaps I wouldn't react so immediately strongly. I knew that this person probably wouldn't respond well but did it anyways.

What did you learn from this experience?

I learned a bit about how this person thinks and responds to confrontation. I also learned how much of a useful ally that the other friend was.
